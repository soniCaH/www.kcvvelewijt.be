Phase 5 · Round 1 · Drill #1784 · 5.d2

Subject avatar vocabulary — photo / monogram / illustration / mixed

Drill resolves the avatar fill used inside <QARow> (interview Q&A row, ~32px) and <PullQuote> attribution (all variants, ~64px). The same speaker often appears multiple times in a single article — the chosen vocabulary applies everywhere a subject's avatar renders, not just one site.

Each option renders four sample subjects: Lars (player · psdImage), Wim (staff · photo), An (custom · own photo), Anouk (subject without a photo — the ~10% case per feedback_subject_photo_fallback). Two scales per option so scaling-down behavior is visible.

Vocabulary deltas mapped per option. See compare.md in this folder.

Option A · Circular photo crop
Circle clip from player.psdImage (with Phase 4.5 R9 photo treatment)
Bounded-box circular clip of the existing subject photo. Newsprint filter + paper grain identical to <TapedFigure>. Thin 1px ink border. Fallback for ~10% of subjects without a photo is the monogram from Option B — but the primary vocabulary is photo.
Vocabulary ✓ Existing player.psdImage / staff.photo / customPhoto
✓ R9 newsprint filter
✓ R9 paper grain
Δ Net-new aspect="circle" on TapedFigure (one-line prop value, or a thin <SubjectAvatar> wrapper)
Δ No-photo fallback rule (defer to B monogram)

Option D · Mixed (scale-conditional)
Monogram at Q&A row scale (~32px) · photo at pull-quote scale (~64px)
Same speaker gets two visual treatments depending on context. Rationale: at 32px a photo crop is too small to recognise a face; at 64px a monogram feels generic. Pick the right tool for the scale. Inconsistency hazard: same person reads differently across the same article.
Vocabulary ✓ Both A's photo + B's monogram primitives
✓ Scale-conditional rule keyed off the consuming component
Δ Two avatar primitives in the system instead of one
Δ Same subject ≠ same visual within one article (consistency risk)
