Phase 6.C · Team detail · Round 1 · Drill 6.C.d1

One question: how do you read a team page?

Same content, same retro vocabulary, three information architectures. The data is locked (see data-reality-locked.md): hero, standings, schedule, squad, staff, editorial, global sponsors — all auto-hide when empty (youth teams). Exemplar = KCVV Elewijt A-ploeg, 3e Provinciale A. The only thing being decided here is the arrangement, not the per-component look (standings cells, player cards, hero artefact each get their own later drill).

Variant A — Single-scroll + striped seams  ·  the 6.A / 6.B pattern
Everything stacked top-to-bottom, <StripedSeam> between sections. You scroll the whole story. Empty sections simply vanish — a U8 page is just hero + squad + staff. Matches how player & match detail already read.

Variant B — Reskinned tabs  ·  today's IA, retro paint
Hero stays; below it a <FilterTabs>-style bar swaps one panel at a time. You see one section, click for the next. Dense data feels contained — but a youth team with only a squad shows tabs that lead to empty panels (or tabs must be conditionally hidden). Shown below with Klassement active.
