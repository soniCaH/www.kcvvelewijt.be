The Wedstrijden problem: 40–50 matches a season.

IA stays single-scroll + sticky nav (the A/C blend you liked). An A-team season runs 40–50 fixtures (league + beker + oefenmatchen), so this section is the real stress test. The only open piece is how the Wedstrijden section holds a full season without burying the squad/staff below it. Four treatments below, each the same KCVV A-ploeg season. The fourth is the §6.6 newspaper-agenda style — which also previews the parked 6.D kalender direction.

Treatment 1 — Teaser on page  →  dedicated full-schedule view
Team page carries only next + recent + a big "Volledige kalender" link. The full season lives on its own scrollable page (shown dashed below). Team page stays lean; full matches becomes its own destination.

Treatment 2 — Month-grouped accordion, inline
All ~46 stay on the team page. Grouped by month (Aug→Jun); past months collapsed (click to open), current + upcoming open by default. Try clicking the months — note how many months a full A-team season spans.

Treatment 4 — §6.6 newspaper agenda  ·  (also previews 6.D kalender)
No cards. Month headings + dashed-divider rows: date · fixture (+ competition/venue) · result. Tabular, dense, editorial — a whole season reads compactly. This is the master-plan §6.6 vocabulary; seeing it here is a free taste of the kalender direction.

Decide

Pick a Wedstrijden treatment (1 / 2 / 3 / 4). They're not mutually exclusive with the agenda question for 6.D — if you love treatment 4 here, that's a strong signal the kalender should go agenda too. Per-row styling (shields, result emphasis) is a later detail; this is about structure.
