Decision 7o3 — reconsidered at ~25 nodes / 4 levels

A the official collapsible chart — reads when one branch is open, pans when fully expanded; doubles as a print view. B ★ spotlight — the only one that looks identical at 25 or 250 and the only genuinely good phone experience; trades away “whole shape at once”. C radial — gorgeous as a whole, but 20 outer labels collide and 4-level depth needs a B-style zoom underneath. D ◎ 3-D clustered — memorable, helped by depth-grouping, but heaviest and label-fragile; best as an enhancement, not the base.

At real scale the field narrows to a navigable base (B) ± a whole-shape view (A or C) ± a 3-D toggle (D). Recommended: B as the base (scale-proof, mobile, oriented), with A available as the “volledig organigram” / print view, and D’s 3-D as the opt-in ◎ enhancement. But you may value C’s whole-club figure enough to make it the headline — your call. Tell me the base + which extras, and I’ll lock the combo (3-D stays its own build phase).
