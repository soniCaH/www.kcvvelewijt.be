Primitive mapping (no new vocabulary)

  • Kicker → <MonoLabel variant="plain"> (jersey-deep raw label-token span on cream; cream on dark).
  • Headline → <EditorialHeading level=1> — Freight, upright, italic jersey-deep/warm accent word + warm "." terminator.
  • Lead → italic font-display (auto-hides when absent — see club/[slug] + scheurkalender).
  • Photo → <TapedFigure> newsprint (colour, not greyscale) + one tape strip; no photo → text-only (A) / <JerseyShirt> (B) / text-only (C).

Trade-offs

  • A cream-minimal — calmest, perfect for plain/CMS pages; but light presence on marquee pages, and the photo reads as "below the title" rather than part of the hero.
  • B dark band — strongest marquee presence + best photo pop; but a dark band on every interior page (incl. utility ones) can feel heavy/repetitive, and it's the closest to the legacy dark hero it replaces.
  • C cream paper-card — most retro-terrace character, one shape handles image/no-image; but a bordered card hero is the heaviest chrome and must not read as just another content card.
ROUND 1 — pick the voice. Which chrome register should the generic page-hero adopt? (A cream-minimal · B dark band · C cream paper-card · or a hybrid — e.g. "C with a register prop that flips to B's dark fill for marquee pages".) Once locked, Round 2 drills the real fields + image rules.
