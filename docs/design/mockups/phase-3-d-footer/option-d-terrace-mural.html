PHASE 3 · CHECKPOINT D · SITEFOOTER · DIRECTION D ★ TERRACE MURAL 2026-05-03

Een terras-muurschildering.

Direction D pushes the footer the furthest from the retro-terrace-fanzine screenshot. The footer is reframed as a layered scrapbook mural — torn-paper edges, overlapping TapedCards at small rotations, a partial jersey-deep wash, a hand-painted-style "1932" stamp, and information distributed across the collage rather than ranked into columns. This is the highest-risk option of the four: more compositional density, more room for visual mistakes, and the largest departure from Phase 1/2 chrome density. Worth it if the owner wants the footer to feel like a stadium-tunnel mural rather than a sitemap.

★ 02 · SECTIONS BREAKDOWN — per-panel role + risk/distinctiveness tradeoff

Wat elk stuk doet.

The mural is not a grid — it is a deliberately staged collage. Each panel earns its rotation and overlap by carrying a specific role. Below: what each piece is, and the honest cost of placing it that way.

★ JERSEY WASH + 1932 STAMP

De ankerkleur.

A partial jersey-deep wash on the left third gives the mural its terrace-tunnel feel — a single saturated brand colour against the rest of the cream paper. The "1932" stamp anchors that column with a hand-cut polygon edge and a Playfair italic year set at 132px. Founding year as identity, not as small-print legal.

↑ FOR

The single brand-saturated zone reads instantly; no other footer in the design system uses jersey-deep at this scale. The 1932 stamp is the most ownable element in the entire mural.

↓ AGAINST

A partial colour wash is harder to maintain than full-bleed ink — torn-edge polygons need careful per-breakpoint tuning. Risks "decorative" reading if not tied to real content (the crest card on top of it is the bridge).

★ NAV TAPEDCARD

De sitemap als briefje.

Ten primary links live inside one TapedCard tilted -2°, two columns of italic Playfair links, masking-tape strips top-left and top-right. The card sits over the boundary between the jersey wash and the cream — the most prominent panel in the mural by surface area.

↑ FOR

Putting the entire nav inside one tilted card is the clearest single-element distinctiveness move available — no competitor footer treats nav as collage paper. Reuses TapedCard verbatim, no new primitive.

↓ AGAINST

A tilted nav card is a screen-reader / focus-ring liability if rotation isn't disabled at prefers-reduced-motion + small viewports. Two-column layout inside a rotated container can drift visually under long localisation strings.

★ CONTACT TAPEDCARD

Het adresboekje.

Address, mail, telefoon, kantine-uren rendered as a definition list inside a TapedCard tilted +3°. Center-tape strip. Sits in the right-third of the mural, slightly lower than the nav card so the two never overlap — they read as two pinned notes.

↑ FOR

Definition-list semantics are correct (<dl>/<dt>/<dd>) and the rotated card is tasteful — only +3°. Keeps contact info easy to scan despite the playful framing.

↓ AGAINST

Two rotated cards in close proximity (-2° + +3°) is the maximum the rotation budget should ever spend. If a third card is added later it must stay flat (0°) or the mural starts to read as a hot mess.

★ TORN-EDGE PHOTO SCRAP

De terras-foto.

A 4:3 rectangular bounded-box photo, masking-tape pinned, with an irregular clip-path simulating a torn edge. Default caption is a tribune snapshot — easy to swap to any approved photo from the asset library. Tilted -4° (the rotation maximum).

↑ FOR

Rectangular photo respects the saved-memory default (~90% of player photos arrive as rectangular psdImage). Torn-edge clip-path is geometric, not blurred — stays inside the flat paper-tone constraint.

↓ AGAINST

Editorial photos must be hand-picked per page — a wrong photo (eg. a generic stock terrace shot) collapses the mural's authenticity. Likely needs a curated rotating set of 4–8 approved photos per season, owned by the marketing lead.

★ SOCIAL TICKETSTUB

De volg-strookje.

Five social platforms inside a TicketStub at -2°. Each glyph is a 36×36 ink-bordered cream square that uses the canonical press-down hover (translate(3px, 3px) + shadow-none + jersey-bright fill on hover). Typographic glyphs only — never the platform logo lockups.

↑ FOR

TicketStub for "ticket to follow the team" reads as an obvious metaphor without needing a label. Glyphs over icons matches the saved-memory feedback (typographic glyphs over Lucide equivalents). Hover canon respected.

↓ AGAINST

Typographic glyphs for social platforms (especially X and Instagram) are recognisable but not platform-trademark-safe. Some users may not parse "◉" as Instagram immediately. Counter: every glyph carries an aria-label; recognition reads on hover via the press idiom.

★ MOTTO BANNER + BOTTOM STRIP

De gestabiliseerde bodem.

Two flat elements anchor the mural — the ink motto banner (with the verbatim "ER IS MAAR ÉÉN PLEZANTE COMPAGNIE" claim, jersey-bright ÉÉN), and the legal/colophon strip beneath it. These never rotate, never tear. They are the line that stops the collage drifting off the page.

↑ FOR

Every collage needs a steadying baseline. Two flat ink elements give the mural a clear "gallery wall" termination. Legal links remain accessible at full mono-caps clarity, no rotation, no overlap.

↓ AGAINST

Putting the motto on ink + the strip on ink stacks two dark bands at the bottom — visually heavy at narrow widths. The torn polygon clip on the motto banner is the only thing keeping them visually distinct. If the polygon is dropped for performance or accessibility reasons, the two bands fuse into a wall.

★ 03 · PRIMITIVE REUSE MAP — this option pulls heavily on existing primitives

Wat al bestaat.

The Terrace Mural looks the most novel of the four directions, but it is built almost entirely from primitives already approved in Phases 1 and 2 — TapedCard, TapeStrip, TicketStub, StampBadge, MonoLabel, EditorialHeading. The novelty is in composition density, not in new tokens. That's the bet.

Mural panel Primitive(s) Pull intensity New surface required?
NAV card TapedCard + TapeStrip ×2 HIGH None — rotation prop already supported.
CONTACT card TapedCard + TapeStrip (center) HIGH None.
SOCIAL stub TicketStub + 5× ink-bordered glyph buttons HIGH One new atom — SocialGlyphButton 36×36, but it's just a press-down paper-card mini already on main.
1932 stamp StampBadge + clip-path torn polygon MEDIUM Stamp size (280×220) is bigger than today's StampBadge default — needs a size="founding" variant. Polygon clip-path is data, not a new primitive.
Crest card TapedCard (rotated, no tape) + EditorialHeading HIGH None.
Photo scrap TapedFigure + torn clip-path MEDIUM TapedFigure already exists; this adds an optional edge="torn" prop carrying the polygon. One prop, no new component.
Motto banner Custom — ink bg + Playfair italic + torn edges LOW New element <FooterMottoBanner>, footer-scoped only. Reuses the same italic display + torn-polygon technique as the stamp, so no new visual language.
Jersey wash + torn top edge Background + clip-path LOW Footer-internal only. Implemented with two pseudo-elements on .footer-stage — no exported primitive.
Bottom strip Plain ink bar + mono caps HIGH None — same chrome as SiteHeader dark variant.

Pull-intensity summary: 5 of 9 panels are pure existing-primitive composition; 2 panels add a single variant prop to an existing primitive; 2 panels (motto banner, jersey wash) are footer-internal CSS — they never leave the footer file. No new tokens. No new fonts. No new colours beyond what Phase 1/2 already shipped.

↑ FOR

The mural is the highest-distinctiveness footer in the four-option set, but its token cost is zero — every effect is composition, not new primitives. If the owner picks Direction D, the design system grows by one variant prop on StampBadge and one on TapedFigure. No new colours, no new fonts, no new shadows.

↓ AGAINST

Composition density is itself a maintenance cost. The mural needs an explicit "do not add a fourth tilted card" rule in the footer README, otherwise future contributors will tilt every new panel and the collage degrades into chaos. Highest design risk in the set — if the rotations / overlaps drift even 1–2px off intent, the whole footer reads as broken rather than playful.

★ 04 · DIRECTION-LEVEL TRADEOFFS — honest cost/value of picking the most experimental option

Waarom (of niet) deze richting.

Direction D is the swing-for-the-fences choice. If picked, the footer becomes a signature element of the redesigned site — the place where the "retro-terrace fanzine" thesis is loudest. If a more conservative footer is preferred, A / B / C all carry the same content with less visual risk.

↑ FOR

Maximum distinctiveness in the four-option set. Communicates club identity (1932, motto, address, terras) before communicating sitemap. No competitor club site in the inspiration set (Sporting Hasselt, Real Madrid, Inter Miami CF) treats the footer as a mural — Direction D is the only option that makes the footer a destination rather than a wayfinding utility. Token cost is zero. Press-down hover canon respected. Motto verbatim. Stenciletta avoided. Jersey-deep used as content colour, not chrome glow. Photo defaults rectangular per saved-memory. Glyphs over Lucide icons.

↓ AGAINST

Highest visual risk of the set. Three rotated panels close together (stamp -3°, nav -2°, contact +3°, photo -4°, social -2°) is at the absolute ceiling of the rotation budget — one more rotated element and the mural collapses. Mobile rendering must fall back to a stacked, flat layout (handled in the media query above) and that fallback is much closer to a conventional footer — meaning Direction D's distinctiveness only exists at ≥900px viewports. Collage maintenance demands curated photo rotation and a "no fourth tilt" guard in the footer README. Accessibility requires prefers-reduced-motion + focus-ring testing on every rotated card. Highest QA cost of the four options.

★ NOTES — constraints respected, decisions to ratify, mobile fallback

Resolved

  • Motto verbatim: ER IS MAAR ÉÉN PLEZANTE COMPAGNIE with ÉÉN in jersey-bright italic Playfair
  • Founding year framed as identity (1932 stamp), not legal small-print
  • Rotations capped at ±4° everywhere — stamp -3°, nav -2°, contact +3°, photo -4°, social -2°, motto -0.6°
  • Press-down hover canon used on every interactive paper element: translate(3–4px, 3–4px) + shadow-none + transition-all 300ms
  • No glows, no rim-lights — torn edges via clip-path: polygon(...), never blur or filter effects
  • Photos default to rectangular bounded box (4:3) — tracks saved-memory feedback that ~90% of subject photos arrive as psdImage rectangles
  • Social affordances are typographic glyphs in 36×36 paper-card minis — never Lucide nor platform-logo lockups
  • Stenciletta deliberately avoided — display work is Playfair italic 900, body is Inter, mono is IBM Plex Mono
  • Token map reused 1:1 from Phase 2 alert — zero new colours, zero new shadows, zero new fonts
  • Mobile fallback (≤ 900px) flattens the mural into a stacked column; jersey-wash pseudo and torn-top pseudo are dropped — distinctiveness reads on desktop only, mobile reads as a clean conventional footer
  • All rotated panels expose the rotation under a prefers-reduced-motion: reduce media query that resets to 0° (production-only — mockup retains rotation for visual review)

Open

Owner pick between Direction A (the closest-to-screenshot footer), Direction B, Direction C, and Direction D (this — terrace mural). If D is selected, the implementation plan must include: (1) curated photo rotation for the photo scrap (4–8 approved per season), (2) a "no fourth tilt" guard in the footer README, (3) a Storybook story per breakpoint (≥1024 / 768–1023 / ≤767), and (4) a focus-ring audit on every rotated card.

  • Wash column: --color-jersey-deep @ 92% opacity
  • Card surfaces: --color-cream
  • Motto banner: --color-ink with --color-jersey-bright accent
  • Bottom strip: --color-ink
  • Tape colour: warm ochre rgba(247, 230, 130, 0.78) with hairline ink edge — same value used in TapeStrip on main

STRUCTURE / DEPTH

  • 1932 stamp: 3px solid ink + 8×8 ink shadow + torn polygon clip-path
  • Photo scrap: 2px solid ink + --shadow-paper-md + torn polygon clip-path
  • Social glyph buttons: 2px solid ink + 3×3 ink shadow (press → shadow-none + translate(3px, 3px))
  • Sharp corners everywhere — no border-radius except the round ticket-stub punch-holes
