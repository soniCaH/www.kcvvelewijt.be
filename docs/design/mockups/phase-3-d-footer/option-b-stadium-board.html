★ 02 · SECTIONS BREAKDOWN — five horizontal bands, top to bottom, with per-band tradeoffs

Vijf banden, elk een eigen rij.

The footer is composed of five horizontal bands stacked vertically. Each band carries a distinct rhetorical job; together they read as the rolling rows of a stadium scoreboard.

BAND 01 MOTTO TICKER · jersey-green type on ink, looped infinite

Horizontal scrolling band running the club motto verbatim: ER IS MAAR ÉÉN PLEZANTE COMPAGNIE. Repeats with star separators, scrolls left at 40s/cycle. Letter-spacing 0.18em, weight 600, jersey-green on ink. No glow — readability comes from contrast and tracking, not bloom.

↑ FOR

Carries the club voice without claiming a hero position. Sets the dot-matrix register the rest of the footer inherits. Looping animation is the only motion in the slab — gives the board a heartbeat without distracting upward.

↓ AGAINST

Animation in the footer can pull the eye when scrolling past on long pages. Mitigation: prefers-reduced-motion halts the loop. On low-content pages the motto ticker can read as the most active element on screen.

BAND 02 WORDMARK · Freight Display club name + mono meta column

The single editorial moment: KCVV Elewijt in Freight Display 900 with italic Elewijt in jersey-green. To the right, three mono caps lines — live dot + season, address, founding year. Hairline divider underneath.

↑ FOR

Reserves Freight Display for one moment only — the club name. Reinforces that the typeface is editorial, not utility. Live-dot + season label echoes a real scoreboard's "ON AIR" indicator without inventing new chrome.

↓ AGAINST

The Freight Display call-out tonally clashes with the rest of the slab on first read — it's the only non-mono surface in the entire footer. If the user wants the footer to read as purely technical, drop the wordmark and lead with the column board.

BAND 03 FOUR-COLUMN BOARD · STAMNUMMER · CONTACT · TEAMS · SOCIAL

The information core. Four equal columns, each headed by a mono caps label with a hairline jersey-deep underline and a "01 / 04"-style counter on the right. Columns separated by hairline cream dividers at 14% opacity. STAMNUMMER showcases the club's KBVB registration number in a 32px jersey-green block. SOCIAL uses paper-stamped mini-cards inverted on ink (cream border, jersey-deep shadow) and follows the canonical press-down hover.

↑ FOR

Four equal columns mirror a scoreboard's symmetric column layout. Counter glyph ("01 / 04") nods at the dot-matrix register without asking for a new icon set. Stamnummer column gives the footer a unique signal — clubs in Belgium recognise № 5587 immediately. Social column reuses the press-down hover canon, so even on the inverted ink slab the interaction language is unchanged.

↓ AGAINST

Four equal columns under 900px collapse to 2×2 — works, but the visual rhythm of the scoreboard suffers when wrapped. On very dense pages with long sponsor or team lists, columns can become uneven; a future variant may want a fifth "PARTNERS" column or a row-flow.

BAND 04 FIXTURES STRIP · next 3 fixtures, mono date · time · opponent

A label cell ("VOLGENDE DRIE") followed by three fixture cells. Each fixture is three lines: date in mono caps + jersey-green, match line with KCVV in jersey-green emphasis and opponent in cream, venue meta in cream-muted. Faint jersey-deep wash on the band distinguishes it from the columns above. Reads as the live scoreline strip on a real board.

↑ FOR

Gives the footer a job beyond navigation — every page surfaces the next three fixtures. Tightens the loop between the editorial body of the site and the matchday rhythm. Mono date format is unambiguous and reads at a glance.

↓ AGAINST

Adds a data dependency to the footer — every page now requires the next-fixtures BFF query (already cached, but a coupling). When fixtures are absent (off-season, fixture not yet announced), the band needs a graceful empty state. Three fixed slots may feel sparse during weeks with two scheduled matches.

BAND 05 BASE STRIP · copyright + legal links, all mono caps

Single-line strip: copyright + legal links (Privacy, Cookies, Disclaimer, Colofon). 10px mono caps, cream at 70% opacity, links shift to jersey-green on hover. Closes the slab in a single tight line.

↑ FOR

Kept deliberately small and quiet — the legal links never compete with the columns above. Cream-at-70% fades into the slab without disappearing.

↓ AGAINST

10px mono can fail WCAG AA contrast at the 70% opacity setting on the ink slab — needs a contrast pass before production. If GDPR demands a more prominent cookies link, this band becomes the wrong home for it.

★ 03 · PRIMITIVE REUSE MAP — how each band maps back to existing redesign primitives

Welke primitieven dragen het scorebord.

Every visual choice in this footer maps back to a primitive already approved earlier in the redesign series. The slab is not a new aesthetic — it inverts the cream/ink polarity and switches the body face to mono, but every interaction and structural rule is borrowed intact from the existing vocabulary.

↑ FOR

Every band borrows from a primitive already approved earlier in the redesign series — no new atoms invented. The polarity inversion (cream/ink → ink/cream) is the only structural divergence; every interaction rule and typographic token survives intact.

↓ AGAINST

The inversion + heavy reliance on mono moves the footer further from the editorial register of the homepage. Users reading the page top-to-bottom shift from Freight-Display-and-cream into mono-and-ink at the bottom — a sharp tonal pivot. Direction A keeps a paper-warm footer; Direction B owns the technical register.

★ NOTES — token map, structure decisions, production handoff

Resolved

  • Footer slab inherits paper-card vocabulary inverted — border-2 ink + --shadow-paper-md + sharp corners + ink slab foundation
  • Foreground type is jersey-green + cream only — no new colour tokens introduced
  • Body is IBM Plex Mono everywhere; Freight Display reserved for the wordmark line only
  • Five bands stacked top-to-bottom: motto ticker · wordmark · 4-column board · fixtures strip · base meta
  • Column headers use mono caps + hairline jersey-deep underline + counter glyph (01 / 04)
  • Social mini-cards use inverted paper-card chrome (cream border + jersey-deep shadow on ink) and the canonical press-down hover
  • Fixtures strip surfaces the next 3 fixtures with mono date · KCVV-in-jersey · opponent-in-cream
  • Hairline gridlines on the slab (32×32 CSS grid at 4% cream opacity) carry the scoreboard register without pixel-glow effects
  • Club motto verbatim: ER IS MAAR ÉÉN PLEZANTE COMPAGNIE — looped infinite in the top ticker
  • No glows, no rim-lights, no Stenciletta — the dot-matrix vibe is type + spacing only

Open

Confirm fixtures-strip empty-state behaviour for off-season weeks. Confirm WCAG AA contrast on the 10px base-strip type.
