PHASE 3 · B FOLLOW-UP · HOMEPAGE PLACEMENT ★ EDITORIALHERO · placement="homepage" 2026-05-05

Het hero op de homepage.

The locked detail-page hero (Asymmetric Broadsheet, 60/40 + ink rule) renders without click-through CTAs — the reader is already on the article. Homepage placement adds a click-through affordance so the same hero can act as a featured-article teaser at the top of the homepage's news section. One drill question, three CTA strategies.

★ Q0 — CONTEXT AUDIT
  • Same shell, different placement. The placement="homepage" extension reuses the locked Asymmetric Broadsheet shell verbatim (60/40 grid · ink rule · TapedCard cover image · Playfair italic 900 heading · jersey-deep accent on the title's accent decorator span). Variant-specific artefacts (eventFact strip, transferFact strip, Q&A divider in interview) do not render in homepage placement — those are body content, not hero content.
  • Single featured slot. Homepage shows one EditorialHero teaser at the top of its news section; other articles render as smaller preview cards (Phase 4 homepage rebuild owns those). The drill below is for the single featured slot.
  • Click destination. The CTA always links to /nieuws/{article.slug} regardless of variant. No variant-specific routes.
  • Reading-time + publishedAt stay in the kicker (already there in the detail spec); they're equally meaningful as teaser metadata.

↑ FOR

Cleanest possible look — same chrome as the detail-page hero, zero added affordances at rest. Press-up hover (translate -2px,-2px + grown shadow) is the inverse of the canonical press-down hover used on paper-stamped buttons; signals "lift / pick up" rather than "press down". Hover hint appears only on focus/hover so the card stays editorial-clean by default. Pattern is widely understood (entire teaser cards are clickable everywhere on the web).

↓ AGAINST

No explicit affordance for non-hover devices — touch users get no visible "click me" signal at all (hover hint never appears). Users with reduced-motion preferences may not see the lift-on-hover feedback. Selecting text inside the hero (e.g. copying the lead) becomes harder because the whole block is a link. A11y: harder to communicate "this whole card navigates to X" via screen reader without adding an aria-label wrapping the entire hero.
