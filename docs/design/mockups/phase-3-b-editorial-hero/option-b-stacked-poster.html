PHASE 3 · CHECKPOINT B · EDITORIAL HERO · DIRECTION B ★ STACKED POSTER 2026-05-03

Een fly-poster aan de muur.

Direction B reads the EditorialHero as a stacked poster: kicker on top, then a single full-bleed display headline that fills the whole poster width, then a horizontal artefact band below the headline (not beside it), then dek + CTA. Variant only changes what lives inside the band — the hero shell, the kicker, the headline, the dek, and the CTA row are invariant.

★ 01 · TRANSFER — before/after shields, jersey-deep tint, signing announcement

Variant transfer.

Player signs from another club. Band shows two side-by-side shields with the arrow between, the from club tinted muted and the to club tinted jersey-soft. Reads as a transfer market notice, not a news article.

↑ FOR

Stacked poster gives the headline maximum room — "Welkom, Maxim Breugelmans" reads like a tannoy announcement, not a card title. Transfer band is unambiguously a transfer (two shields + arrow) so the variant reads at a glance even on small screens. Jersey-deep tint on the inbound side carries the brand colour without painting the whole hero green.

↓ AGAINST

Display-2xl headline pushes the artefact band well below the fold on short viewports. Two shields + arrow is a wide composition; below ~480px wide the band has to reflow vertically and the arrow becomes a chevron-down — that reflow rule must ship as a tested case in Storybook.

↑ FOR

Flat key/value strip is the most data-dense way to fit four match facts inline — kickoff, venue, competition, date — without drowning the headline. Could be mitigated by treating the ticket stub as decorative and routing all clicks to the foot row.

↓ AGAINST

Two PlayerFigure thumbnails at this size leave very little room for any third or fourth subject (trio interviews exist). Three subjects need a different band layout (3-up at smaller scale) — that's a second sub-variant of the interview band, not free reuse. Below ~480px the ampersand has to rotate 90° (becomes a vertical separator), which feels less editorial than the desktop composition.

↑ FOR

Photo-forward band gives event hosts the surface they want — a poster crop where their key image actually breathes. Date marker in mono + display italic mirrors the matchday clock and reuses the perforated stub language from TicketStub.

↓ AGAINST

Event photos are the lowest-quality assets in the system (phone snaps, scanned flyers). The full-bleed crop ruthlessly exposes weak source material. Ships with a fallback rule: if no usable image, replace event-poster with a typographic poster card (heavy display headline + date marker only) rather than a placeholder.

↑ FOR

StampBadge is already in the catalogue — reuse, not reinvention. A rotated stamp gives the announcement weight without resorting to a colour banner or photographic device. The supporting MonoLabelRow communicates the meta (date, effective scope, signatory) flatly.

↓ AGAINST

One central stamp + a row of mono labels can read sparse for a hero band — there's a lot of cream paper around the stamp. Risk of feeling under-engineered next to the photo-forward variants on the same homepage. Mitigation: ship a "with portrait" sub-variant where the stamp pairs with a TapedCard PlayerFigure (e.g. for personal endorsements).

★ 06 · GENERIC — typographic drop-cap mark + tape ornament fallback

Variant generic.

The catch-all. Used when the article doesn't fit any other variant — a summary, an opinion piece, a "wist je dat". Band is purely typographic: an oversized italic drop-cap mark, a dashed rule, a tape ornament. No imagery is required.

NIEUWS CLUB 04 MIN

leerden in oktober.

5.

Een seizoenshelft op kruissnelheid. Wat de B-ploeg ons vertelde over het opleidingsmodel, waarom de jeugdwerking ineens dieper graaft, en het ene detail in de kantine dat alles veranderde.

DEEL ARTIKEL LEES VOLLEDIG →

↑ FOR

Zero imagery dependency — generic articles often arrive without art direction. The drop-mark + dashed rule + tape composition is dense enough to feel intentional, light enough to ship in five minutes from text alone. Reuses TapeStrip vocabulary as decoration without compromising the poster shell.

↓ AGAINST

Without imagery the variant leans 100% on typographic charisma — needs the display headline to actually pop. A bland headline ("clubnieuws") + this band lands flat. Recommend pairing this variant with editorial copy guidelines (verb-led headlines, short numerals or pronouns up top).

Ships with explicit breakpoint specs (≥1024 / ≥720 / <720).

★ 08 · MOBILE COLLAPSE — stacked column already, padding tightens, headline drops to display-lg

Op mobiel.

The whole point of stacked-poster is that the desktop layout is already a single column — the kicker, headline, band, dek, CTAs are stacked top to bottom by default. Mobile only tightens spacing, drops the display-2xl headline to ~44px, and reflows the artefact band into a vertical mini-stack.

★ 09 · PRIMITIVE REUSE MAP — every artefact mapped back to a catalogue primitive

Reuse contract.

Every visual choice in every variant traces back to a primitive that's already in the catalogue or already shipping. EditorialHero introduces zero new primitives — only new compositions of existing ones inside the artefact band.

↑ FOR

One discriminated variant prop on a single component. Shell (kicker, headline, dek, CTA row) is invariant across all 7 variants — only the band slot differs. Storybook ships one story per variant + autodocs + VR per variant; ~7 stories total.

Mitigation: extract each band as its own sibling component in EditorialHero/bands/<Variant>Band.tsx, keep EditorialHero/index.tsx as a thin switch.

★ 10 · DIRECTION TRADEOFFS — summary across all variants and viewports

Waarom deze richting.

Direction B treats the hero as a single fly-poster pasted on a wall — headline lives at the top, big and unchallenged. The artefact band is a horizontal stripe under the title rather than a sidebar — gives every variant the same vertical rhythm and removes the "left column / right column" tension entirely.

↑ FOR

Headline gets full poster width — display-2xl reads enormous at any breakpoint and the variant prop never steals room from it. Mobile is essentially "just tighter" — no two-column-to-one reflow rules to debug per variant. Every variant has the same vertical signature (kicker → head → band → foot) so the homepage rhythm is predictable even when six variants stack one after the other. Reuses the entire primitive catalogue (TapedCard, TicketStub, MonoLabel, StampBadge, ClippedCard, PlayerFigure, TapeStrip) verbatim — no new chrome.

↓ AGAINST

Pushes the artefact below the fold on shorter viewports. The two-column "left-words / right-artefact" tension that the master redesign doc calls "universal" (§5.4) is deliberately abandoned here — that tension is what carries the duo-interview hero in the source mockups, and it doesn't survive the stack. Variant differentiation now relies entirely on the band slot — if a viewer skims the kicker and headline alone, they have no other variant cue. Risk of a homepage that reads as "seven near-identical poster shells with different stripes underneath".

★ NOTES — production handoff for the EditorialHero stacked-poster direction

Resolved

  • Single component <EditorialHero variant=...> with 7 discriminated variants — invariant shell, swappable artefact band
  • Shell primitives: MonoLabelRow kicker, EditorialHeading size="display-2xl", dek paragraph, CTA row with Button primary + ghost
  • Artefact band sits below the headline, full poster width, bordered top + bottom 2px ink with dashed rule overlay
  • Press idiom on every interactive primitive: hover:shadow-none + translate(4px, 4px) + transition-all 300ms ease
  • Subject photos default rectangular (event poster, transfer-card branding); circular crops only inside PlayerFigure
  • No radial glows, no rim-lights — paper shadows only
  • Glyph language uses Playfair italic numerals / arrows / ampersands; Lucide icons only when no typographic alternative exists
  • Mobile collapse is single-axis — only padding, type scale, and band internal grid change; the parent shell never reflows
  • Each band sub-renderer extracted to EditorialHero/bands/<Variant>Band.tsx to keep index.tsx a thin switch
  • Storybook: one story per variant (tags: ["autodocs", "vr"]) — VR catches regressions across all 7

Open

Owner pick between Direction B (this), Direction A (side-by-side two-column), and any sibling directions. If B is locked: write EditorialHero in apps/web/src/components/design-system/EditorialHero/, retire the existing PageHero, migrate the homepage, interview detail, event detail, and player profile templates.

Token map

SHELL / SURFACE

  • Hero surface: --color-cream
  • Hero border: 2px solid var(--color-ink)
  • Hero shadow: --shadow-paper-md (6×6 ink)
  • Headline colour: --color-ink with italic emphasis in --color-jersey-deep
  • Underline-highlight: --color-jersey at ~55% alpha behind word
  • Dek colour: --color-ink-soft
  • Kicker bg: --color-cream-soft with 1.5px ink border

BAND / ARTEFACT

  • Band borders: 2px ink top + bottom + dashed 6px overlay rule
  • TapedCard rotation-A: rotate(-1.4deg); rotation-B: rotate(1.6deg)
  • TapeStrip tints: jersey @ 45%, ochre @ 45%, ink @ 60%
  • StampBadge sizes: md=152px, lg=200px, default rotation -8deg
  • TicketStub: 12px perforation circles at edge midpoints
  • PlayerFigure sizes: md=160px, lg=220px, 3px ink border
  • Press idiom across all primitives: translate(4px, 4px) + shadow-none on hover
