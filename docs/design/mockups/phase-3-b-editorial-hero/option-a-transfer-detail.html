★ 04 · A/B PULL-QUOTE — TRADE-OFF

Welke quote-plaatsing?

CriterionA — In-heroB — Below hero (PullQuote primitive)
Visual weight of the quote Smaller — sits flush with the lead, single column Larger — full prose width, dedicated card, paper rotation, jersey-tone
Hero density Heavier — kicker + heading + lead + quote + byline in one column Lighter — kicker + heading + lead + byline (matches announcement)
Component reuse Requires a small new sub-component (a quote line inside the hero) Reuses existing <PullQuote tone="jersey"> primitive verbatim
Consistency across variants Hero shape diverges from announcement (which has no quote slot) Hero shape stays identical to announcement; quote is a sibling block
Quote prominence Reader sees it before scrolling — high attention Reader sees it after scrolling past the strip — medium attention
When the editor leaves the quote empty Hero collapses cleanly (no row renders) Section is just absent — body flows directly from strip to first paragraph
Risk: fight with cover image None — quote is in the left column, image in the right None — quote sits below both
Mobile collapse Quote stays inline below the lead (one extra line in the stack) Quote sits as a block between strip and body
Editorial voice Quiet — feels like a footnote on the hero Loud — reads as a signature moment, the way a magazine pull-quote does

★ My pick: B. The <PullQuote> primitive already exists, already has the right typographic weight, already supports tone variants. Hero stays consistent with announcement. The quote earns its keep as a paper-card moment instead of a hero footnote. But A is a defensible "quieter" call — confirm or override.
