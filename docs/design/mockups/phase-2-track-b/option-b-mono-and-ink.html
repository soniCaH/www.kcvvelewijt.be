01 · ATOM · SPINNER

Een rustige laad­indicator.

Two sub-variants share one identity. B.1 is the canonical loading state: a quietly rotating 2 px ink arc behind a mono-caps LADEN. ellipsis. B.2 is a three-dot inline pulse for compact contexts — row meta, list footers, micro-loading. No paper texture, no stripes, no rotation of the label itself.

FOR

Reads as quiet system feedback. The mono-caps label doubles as a status string, accessible without relying on shape language. The inline sub-variant avoids the awkward "tiny spinning logo in a list row" mismatch of monolithic spinner systems.

AGAINST

At a glance, almost identical to the React Suspense default. Loses the chance to make "waiting for KCVV data" feel like a club moment.

02 · ATOM · BRANDED TABS

Eén lijn, één onderstreping.

Mono caps labels, generous gap, a single 1 px ink-muted hairline under the row. The active tab is signalled by a 3 px jersey-deep underline that overlaps the hairline by one pixel — no badges, no shadows, no surrounding borders. Hover lifts the muted label into full ink.

FOR

The 3 px jersey-deep stripe is the only club-coded mark on the row. It overlaps the hairline so the underline reads as a continuous member of the same axis — not as an applied decoration. Quiet, but unmistakable.

AGAINST

Without a brand mark or kit motif, the row could live on any modern site. Compared to Direction A's tape and Direction C's heavy underscore, B feels generic at first glance.

03 · ATOM · FILTER TABS

Sharp chips, no curves.

Inactive chips render as transparent text-only — no border, no fill. Active chips flip to bg-ink text-cream in a sharp rectangle (no rounded corners). Counts sit inline behind a 1 px hairline divider — never as pills or badges.

Two scroll arrows: 40 × 40 px squares with a 1 px ink border, a typographic chevron (‹ / ›) in IBM Plex Mono, no fill. Hover inverts to ink-fill / cream-glyph. Track gap matches body grid (16 px).

06 · TOKEN MAP

Wat we wel en niet gebruiken.

The full Phase 2 token surface is available, but Direction B is deliberately narrow: cream surfaces, ink type, ink-muted secondaries, jersey-deep accent. Paper shadow tokens are not consumed.

Tokens explicitly NOT consumed in Direction B --shadow-paper-sm · --shadow-paper-md · --shadow-paper-lg · --shadow-paper-tape · all rotation transforms · the sticker/tape decorative layer.
Direction B's argument: chrome that doesn't cast a shadow lets editorial primitives (TapedCard, EditorialHeading, PullQuote) keep ownership of the paper metaphor. Atoms stay on the page surface.
FOR — overall direction

Cleanest typographic hierarchy of the three options. Editorial primitives have maximum room to breathe. Accessibility passes trivially: every state is colour-and-text, never shape-only.

AGAINST — overall direction

Visually under-articulated. Without paper shadow, tape, or rotation, the atoms don't telegraph "KCVV fanzine" on their own — they rely on neighbouring editorial primitives to do that work. Risk: at small viewports, the page reads as minimalist-default rather than club-coded.
