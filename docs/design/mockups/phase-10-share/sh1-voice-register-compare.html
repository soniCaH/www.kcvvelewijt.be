Primitive mapping (no net-new vocabulary)

  • Kicker → <MonoLabel variant="plain"> — IBM Plex Mono, jersey-deep on cream / warm on dark.
  • Headline → <EditorialHeading> — Freight Display, italic accent + warm "." terminator (replaces Quasimoda all-caps).
  • Score / shirt number → <NumberDisplay> — big Freight numerals; number in a jersey-deep / warm disc.
  • Photo → <TapedFigure> newsprint (colour, not greyscale) + one <TapeStrip>.
  • Seam → <StripedSeam> barber-pole band (A footer accent · B halftone · D the zone split).
  • Square corners throughout; ink borders + hard offset shadow on every framed element; paper-grain texture overlay.

Trade-offs

  • A cream fanzine sheet — calmest, most "fanzine", photo reads as a pasted-in polaroid; but lightest feed presence and the least like a bold match-moment shout.
  • B jersey-deep poster — strongest feed/stories presence, best photo pop, closest heir to today's dark cards; but a dark frame on every moment can feel heavy, and is the furthest from the cream-paper redesign elsewhere.
  • C pinned memorabilia card — purest retro-terrace character, one shape handles every event, looks like a collectible; but the inset margin spends scarce Story real-estate and shrinks the content area.
  • D diagonal-seam programme — keeps the two-zone rhythm fans recognise from today's torn cards, re-expressed in retro vocabulary; but it's the most structurally constrained and the seam angle must survive html-to-image capture cleanly.

Sentiment note (deferred to Round 2)

Today's cards encode good-news vs bad-news (KCVV goal vs opponent goal; our card vs their card) via palette — bright green vs muted/dark. The retro re-encoding (warm/jersey-deep = positive, ink/brick = sober) is a Round 2 decision; Round 1 shows positive moments only so the frame is the only variable.
