CareerLogTable — anchor-row emphasis (+ keep / drop)

Brief Q2 originally framed this as three row-emphasis variants assuming the section ships. Reframed: after 6.d3 / 6.d4 dropped two sections, CareerLogTable is the heaviest remaining commitment from the 6.d0 Variant C lock — a new Sanity schema (player.careerLog[]), ~12h editorial work for 24 A-team players, plus club-crest asset gathering for non-KCVV clubs.

Variants A/B/C all ship the section with different row-emphasis treatments (brief's original framing). Variant D drops the section entirely, eliminating the schema, editorial, and asset workload. Each column shows Maxim's 5-station log and the U13 / no-career case (section hides).

Vocabulary map

  • Schema: player.careerLog: array<{yearRange, clubName, clubCrest?, role, badge?}>
  • Each row composes <MonoLabel> + crest <img> + display <EditorialHeading> + optional <MonoLabel variant="pill-…">

Tradeoff

  • Trajectory shape (loops back to KCVV) doesn't read at a glance

  • Δ <CareerLogRow variant="anchor"> renders heavier club-name + bg-cream-deep tint

  • Tint introduces a third surface tone (cream + cream-soft + cream-deep) into one section
Variant D

Drop CareerLog.

No career log on the player profile. Zero schema migration. Zero editorial backlog. Zero club-crest assets needed. Career history goes wherever it belongs — interview articles, club history page, social posts. The page becomes A-team-agnostic.

Scope reduction

  • Remove player.careerLog[] from 6.d0 schema scope
  • Remove ~12h editorial backlog for 24 A-team players
  • Remove club-crest asset gathering (Standard, Genk, Anderlecht, Berchem, etc.)
  • Page becomes: Hero → BioBlock → (RecentMatchesGrid?) → QuotesBlock

Tradeoff

  • Phase 6.A schema migrations drop to zero — biggest scope win yet
  • Cross-age symmetry improves — no A-team-specific narrative content
  • Loses "history of a player" entirely from the profile surface
  • Mockup fidelity vs retro-terrace-fanzine drops further (~40%)
  • "Logboek" framing dies — that section gave the page its name in the canonical mockup
