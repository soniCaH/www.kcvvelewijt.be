Phase 4 · Round 1 · Homepage hero shape

What shape is the homepage hero?

Today the top of / renders <FeaturedArticles>: a 3-article auto-rotating carousel seeded from articles.slice(0, 3) (no editor pick). Phase 3 shipped <EditorialHero placement="homepage"> as a single-article spotlight for one explicitly-chosen slug. This round picks which shape the homepage hero takes. Round 2 then drills how the hero article(s) are selected; Round 3 places the featured event; Round 4 locks the body-section spine on top of all of those.

What's locked above this round. Phase 3 chrome — <MatchStrip>, <SiteHeader>, <SiteFooter> — already sits above and below every variant. Variants below show only the hero region (the band immediately under the chrome). The grid / schedule / youth / shop / sponsors that follow are out-of-frame for this round.
Variant A
Single spotlight
One editor-picked lead article rendered with the full Phase 3 <EditorialHero placement="homepage">: kicker chips, italic display H1, lead sentence, byline, taped 16:9 cover. Whole block is a Link to /nieuws/{slug} with the canonical press-up hover. One article, one moment, one click target.
Tradeoffs + Strongest editorial weight
+ Reuses Phase 3 component as-is
− Requires editor to pick a lead
− Loses 2 of 3 currently-rotated stories

Variant B
Lead + sidebar
Magazine "front page" pattern: one big EditorialHero on the left at ~64% width, two smaller secondary feature cards stacked on the right. Two extra editor-picks (or "next 2 most recent"). Both right-column cards are Links; the lead is the dominant click target.
Tradeoffs + Surfaces 3 stories, none rotating
+ Reads as broadsheet front page
− Needs sidebar card design (new sub-component)
− Three editorial picks per week is more work
