Phase 4 · Round 9c · jerseys.png in retro register

Bringing today's jersey photo into the retro vocabulary

Today's /images/jerseys.png is a clean modern flat-lay of 5 KCVV kits. Below: four ways to keep that image in Phase 4 without breaking the fanzine voice.

Locked. Webshop section is a pointer to an external supplier (no in-site products). The image is identity-level ("these are our kits") not transactional ("these are products you can buy"). All variants below honour that — no specific-product CTAs. Image source: /images/jerseys.png (existing).
T.1
Polaroid · taped paper frame
The image sits inside a polaroid-style TapedFigure: cream paper border, two jersey-tape strips, slight rotation, italic caption. The photo is unmodified — fully visible at full clarity. The frame brings it into the retro vocabulary.
Tradeoffs + Image fully visible, undistorted
+ Reuses TapedFigure (consistent with NewsCard)
− Photo modernity contrasts with cream/ink palette
− Polaroid framing makes it feel "pinned up" rather than current

T.2
Section backdrop · blur + jersey-deep gradient (YouthBackdrop pattern)
Image fills the full section background, blurred 3px and darkened, with a jersey-deep gradient overlay (88/78/95). Photo becomes atmospheric texture rather than a literal product shot. Same pattern as YouthBlock for visual consistency.
Tradeoffs + Same backdrop pattern as YouthBlock — consistent
+ Photo loses literal product detail (ageing matters less)
− Photo content (5 kits) becomes invisible
− May feel too similar to YouthBlock if both use this treatment

T.3
Halftone duotone print · jersey-deep tinted, dot pattern
Image rendered greyscale + jersey-deep multiply blend + halftone dot overlay. Reads as a printed-newspaper photo. Photo content stays partly visible (shapes still recognisable) but enters the printed-zine register fully. Most fanzine treatment.
Tradeoffs + Strongest fanzine register
+ Photo enters cream/ink palette without breaking voice
− Detail in kits gets sacrificed to dot pattern
− CSS-driven halftone is approximate; may not work on all browsers

T.4
Narrow photo strip · banner card overlapping
The image fills a narrow horizontal band (220px tall, mild grayscale 60% + slight brightness reduction). A taped banner card sits halfway over it, holding the headline + CTA. Image is environmental backdrop; the card is the content. Quiet transition between photo and cream paper below.
Tradeoffs + Image has its own real estate
+ Banner card carries the message
+ Strong layered "magazine spread" feel
− Banner card mid-overlap is unusual; needs care on mobile
