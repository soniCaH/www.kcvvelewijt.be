Photo lift vs canonical press-down — interactive demo.

Three taped news cards, each implementing one of the three hover models. Same card content, same sub-degree rotation, same tape layout. Only the hover transform differs.

How to read this Hover each card. Watch what moves: the CARD (depresses 4px down/right), the PHOTO (lifts 4px up), the TAPE (anchored to card frame in all three; moves with card when card moves). The dashed ghost shows where the card was before hover so the press-down is legible.
Transform values are exaggerated for visibility (4px instead of the production 1–2px) — judge the relative effect, not the exact magnitude.
Variant A · Layered (Recommended)

Card press-down + photo lift

The card depresses canonically (translate down-right). The photo INSIDE the card translates up at the same time. Tape strips stay on the card frame and travel with the card's press-down. Net visual: photo "comes off" the card while the card "sinks into the page."

Variant B · Photo-only lift

Card stays · only photo lifts

Card doesn't move at all on hover. Only the taped photo lifts up. Tape stays put (it's on the card frame, card doesn't move). Deviates from the canonical press-down for this surface specifically — documents an exception.
