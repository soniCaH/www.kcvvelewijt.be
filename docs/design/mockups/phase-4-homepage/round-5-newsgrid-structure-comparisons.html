Phase 4 · Round 5 · NewsGrid structure

How is the NewsGrid laid out?

The IA round locked NewsGrid as the body section after the FeaturedEventBand: 6 article cards, no event slot. This round drills the structural arrangement — number of cards, sizes, and how they tile into the page. Aspect-ratio assignment per card and tape rotation rules drill in subsequent sub-rounds.

Locked from prior rounds. NewsGrid receives 6 articles (articles.slice(3, 9) — skipping the 3 hero articles). Aspect-ratio variants available: landscape-16-9 · square · portrait-3-4 · text-only. Below: 4 structural arrangements, all rendered with realistic Sanity-shape data.
N.1
Strict 3×2 uniform
6 equal cards in a 3-column, 2-row grid. All 16:9. Tape rotation alternates per card (a/b/c/d) so the grid feels hand-pasted, not mechanical, but the structure is predictable.
Tradeoffs + Most predictable for editors
+ Trivial responsive collapse (3→2→1)
− Loses magazine rhythm
− Aspect-ratio prop barely used (only 16:9)

N.2
Broadsheet · lead + 5 supporting
One large lead card on the left (2 col × 2 row, 16:9 large image, bigger headline). Right side: 5 stacked smaller cards (mix of 16:9 and text-only). Reads as a printed front page below the masthead.
Tradeoffs + Strong editorial weight on the lead
+ Full aspect-ratio prop usage
− Lead repeats the EditorialHero's "spotlight one article" pattern
− 5 stacked cards on right tightens text

N.3
Asymmetric editorial · woven 12-col
12-column grid woven with mixed aspects. 1 portrait (3:4, spans 4 cols × 2 rows), 2 squares (4 cols each), 3 landscape (4 cols each). Every aspect-ratio variant gets used at least once; the grid feels hand-set.
Tradeoffs + Most magazine-feeling cadence
+ Exercises every aspect variant
− Slot positions dictate aspect (less editor flexibility)
− Responsive collapse needs care

N.4
Mixed 2-row · 2 large + 4 small
