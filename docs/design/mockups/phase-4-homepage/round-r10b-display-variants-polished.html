Phase 4.5 · R10b · Display variants — polished compositions

Three display variants for image-less cards.

Polished compositions for the cards where a photograph isn't the right answer: event = ticket stub, transfer = jersey-number paper stamp, column = newspaper pull-quote. Each is shown at full NewsCard scale (340px wide) so you can judge whether they actually shine vs the all-photo Option 1.

These compositions reuse retro-fanzine vocabulary across the site: the event stub borrows ticket-perforation geometry, the transfer stamp uses the same number-disc treatment as the future Phase 5 squad surfaces, the column variant uses horizontal stripes (the same pattern R7 considered for meta accents but rejected on news cards — used here as a content- register signal, not a subtle accent).

If these shine for you, Option 2 wins. If they feel like work, Option 1 wins.

Option 2 (with these compositions) adds a `displayMode` prop on `` with at least three sub-templates: ticket, transfer-stamp, column-pull. Each consumes article-type-specific fields (event start time / venue · transferFact playerName/direction/number · article pull-quote/author).

Implementation cost is real — each sub-template is ~30-60 lines of TSX + a story + a VR baseline. Editor workflow also expands: editors decide per-article whether to surface a photo or a display variant. The payoff is the kind of editorial variety that made the source design feel hand-typeset rather than templated.

If photographs are typically available and good-quality, Option 1 is the right call — the photos themselves carry the visual variety. If photographs are often missing / weak / awkward (the flag you raised at the start of the photo-treatment thread), Option 2 hedges by giving content-specific display variants that don't depend on photo quality.
