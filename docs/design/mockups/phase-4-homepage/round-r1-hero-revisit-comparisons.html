Phase 4.5 · Round R1 · Hero placement revisit

Carousel of three, or the brief's split layout?

The Phase 4 hero locked an auto-rotating carousel of 3 featured articles (Round 1 D · Round 1b D.1). The refinement brief drops the carousel and adds a separate "Uitgelicht." row of three prominently-sized featured cards above the news grid. This round revisits that decision with four concrete placement variants and a fixed news-grid phantom underneath each so you can judge how the hero competes with the grid below.

Variants share the retro-fanzine vocabulary: TapedCard, TapeStrip, EditorialHeading (italic serif + jersey-deep accent), MonoLabel, EditorialLead, EditorialKicker.

R1.A — Locked carousel R1.B — Hero + Uitgelicht row R1.C — Lead + 2-stack R1.D — Editorial spread
R1.A · Status quo (Phase 4 Round 1 D · 1b D.1)
Auto-rotating carousel of three
One large hero slot cycles through 3 featured articles every 5s (pauseable, respects prefers-reduced-motion). A strip of 3 thumbnails below shows what's next; click any thumb to jump.
Trade-offs Single attention slot, no clutter Motion telegraphs "this is the marquee" 2 of 3 articles hidden at any moment Carousels are mobile-friction-heavy A11y: auto-rotate needs pause UI
Match-strip + nav · stamped at top — out of round scope

R1.B · The brief's proposal
Single hero + Uitgelicht 3-card row
One large static hero (the most-recently-published featured article), followed by a separate "Uitgelicht." section with three prominently-sized featured cards. Carousel is dropped entirely. Featured semantics live in the row, not in motion.

R1.C · Hybrid
Static lead hero + 2-card "Ook uitgelicht" stack
One large static hero on the left, two mid-sized featured cards stacked on the right. Same vertical footprint as R1.A. All 3 featured articles visible at once, no motion. Closer in spirit to the original retro-fanzine mockups (left-headline / right-photo) but extends the right column to a vertical "featured sidebar."
Trade-offs 3 featured visible, no motion Same vertical footprint as carousel No new section; news grid stays close Right column reads as "sidebar" Only 3 featured slots (vs 4 in R1.B) Smaller cards on right lose some punch
Match-strip + nav · stamped at top — out of round scope

R1.D · Editorial spread
One lead + three sibling-strength featured
A two-column editorial spread: one large lead on the left with a full hero photo, three roughly co-equal featured items on the right (one big-photo card + two text-led headline rows). Mimics a newspaper above-the-fold layout. All 4 featured articles visible at once, hero reads as the marquee but the right column carries real weight.
Trade-offs All 4 featured visible at once Highest density · most editorial energy No motion · no Uitgelicht section needed Right column dense — risks visual noise Two stacked sub-cards lose photographic emphasis Hardest to balance on mobile
Match-strip + nav · stamped at top — out of round scope
