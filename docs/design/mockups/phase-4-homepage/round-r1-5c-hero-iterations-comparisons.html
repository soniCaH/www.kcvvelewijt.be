Phase 4.5 · Round R1.5c · Hero iterations — LANDSCAPE-only

Iterating Interview, Event, and Transfer at hero scale.

Announcement is approved from R1.5b. This page iterates the three remaining variants with three options each. Same hero shell, smaller scale (mini-hero ~235px photo) so we can fit nine mockups comparable side-by-side. Pick one per variant — or call out the bits worth combining.

Constraint (owner, 2026-05-13): all uploaded article hero images (Sanity article.coverImage) are ALWAYS landscape — no portrait or square crops. Mockups in earlier rounds that used 4:5 portrait crops for Interview / Transfer are retired. The right column of the hero ALWAYS renders a landscape taped polaroid.

Person photos (player.psdImage / transparentImage) are separate from the hero image and may appear as small inline credit thumbnails (16px or so) but never as the hero focal point.
Interview · multi-subject Event · venue strip Transfer · chip + pull

Interview.

With landscape-only hero photos, multi-subject articles get ONE landscape cover (article.coverImage, which can be a group shot for duo/panel interviews). The three options vary how the interviewees' NAMES are surfaced in the left column copy.

IV.1 · Joined-names subtitle
Subtitle line below H1
Ports the detail-hero subtitle pattern verbatim: "Met A & B" / "Met A, B en C". Most minimal — one extra row of italic serif beneath the H1.

One chip per interviewee
Chips replace the subtitle line. Each chip carries one interviewee's name in mono-uppercase. Reads more like a "byline strip" — clearer multi-subject signal at a glance.

IV.3 · Credit chips with mini-faces
Tiny Person thumbnails inline with names
Each interviewee's name gets a small 16px square psdImage thumbnail inside its chip (left of the name). Reads as a credit line / cast row. Uses player.psdImage when the subject is a player; falls back to a coloured initial-block otherwise. More informative than IV.2 — visitor sees WHO before reading.

Venue ABOVE the headline
