Phase 4.5 · Round R2 · NewsGrid geometry revisit

Keep the locked 1+4, or switch to a flat grid?

Phase 4 Round 5b locked the news grid as 1 lead spanning 2 rows + 2×2 supporting cluster (`newsgrid-locked.md`). The refinement brief §6 wants 3×2 equal-sized cards — "the variety lives in the cards, not the layout." Round R1.6 just locked Uitgelicht as the prominent 3-up surface above the news grid, which changes the prominence math: the news grid no longer needs internal hierarchy to feel important.

Reminder: all three variants below render the locked Uitgelicht row at the TOP of the surface so you can judge the prominence gradient. Uitgelicht is identical across variants; only the news grid geometry changes.

R2.A · Status quo (Phase 4 Round 5b lock)
1 lead + 4 supporting (asymmetric)
The current shipping geometry. One large lead card spans both rows; four supporting cards form a 2×2 cluster in the right column. Lead card has a bigger H2 (display-md ~26px), 4:3 photo, optional dek. 5 cards total.
Trade-offs No rework — current implementation stays Internal hierarchy = chronological emphasis on most-recent Now redundant with Uitgelicht above (already has a 'feature' surface) Lead-card pattern echoed twice on the page

R2.B · Brief §6 proposal
3×2 equal-sized cards (6 total)
Six chronological news cards in a 3-column × 2-row grid. All cards same size, no lead/supporting hierarchy. Variety lives in the cards themselves (background cycle, tape colours, rotation), not in the layout. Calmer below the more-prominent Uitgelicht.
Trade-offs Clear prominence gradient: Uitgelicht 3-up grand vs news 3×2 small Chronological clarity — no implied priority within news +1 article visible (6 vs 5) Requires NewsGrid component rework Loses the editorial 'lead story' emphasis

R2.C · Alternative geometry
2×3 equal — wider cards in 2 columns
Same 6 cards, but laid out 2 columns × 3 rows. Each card is ~50% width — wider than R2.B's 33% — so each H2 has room to breathe and the dek can render reliably. More like a digital newspaper inner-page than a tile grid.
Trade-offs Each card has more horizontal room — H2 breathes Dek line renders comfortably (1 or 2 lines) Wider cards may compete visually with Uitgelicht above Takes more vertical space than 3×2 (3 rows vs 2)
