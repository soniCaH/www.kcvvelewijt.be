Phase 4.5 · Round R1.6 · Uitgelicht card sizing

Three featured cards. They must read as MORE prominent than the news grid below.

Brief §5 — Uitgelicht is the 3-card featured row that sits between the hero and the news grid. The cards must be visually MORE prominent than the news cards beneath them. Three sizing strategies below: bigger uniform 3-up, asymmetric 1+2 (mini-NewsGrid), and dark contiguous band. A phantom 1+4 news grid (current Phase 4 lock) sits below each so you can judge the prominence gradient.

Cards in all variants use the retro-fanzine card vocabulary: TapedCard, TapedFigure, MonoLabel, EditorialHeading, paper-shadow, warm/jersey tape strips. The variation is sizing + layout, not vocabulary.

R1.6.A — Equal 3-up grand R1.6.B — Asymmetric 1 + 2 R1.6.C — Dark contiguous band
R1.6.A · Equal 3-up grand
Three equally weighted featured cards
All three cards same size, same emphasis. Bigger than the news cards below via: larger H2 (display-md, ~22px vs news card's ~18px display-sm), 16:9 landscape photo at ~340px width, generous padding (lg), and an optional dek (1-line lead preview). Tape strips on each. Reads as "three equally important picks."
Trade-offs No hierarchy decision needed Predictable scale across all featured slots Trivial to map to ARTICLES_QUERY slice [1..3] No "lead featured" emphasis Three roughly-equal cards can feel flat

R1.6.B · Asymmetric 1 + 2
Lead featured + 2 supporting stacked
The most recently published / highest-ranked featured article gets the lead slot: larger card (60% width, display-lg ~28px H2, 4:3 photo at ~480px). The other 2 featured stack vertically on the right (40% width, display-md H2, 16:9 photo). Reads as a mini-NewsGrid one tier above the actual news grid.
Trade-offs Clear hierarchy — top featured carries most weight Strong prominence gradient vs news grid (different layout, not just size) Mirrors the news grid's 1+4 pattern — may feel redundant Lead slot prominence depends on Sanity ordering correctness

R1.6.C · Dark contiguous band
Equal 3-up on a full-bleed jersey-deep section
Three equally weighted cards rendered ON jersey-deep paper (cream text), inside a full-bleed dark green section. The tonal break from the cream homepage body is the prominence signal — Uitgelicht reads as a distinct "this is featured" band, not just a row of bigger cards. Different gradient from news grid via color, not size.
Trade-offs Strongest visual prominence — tonal break is unmissable Echoes the Youth section's dark band rhythm Adds a 3rd full-bleed dark band (after hero/Youth/Clubshop) — may feel heavy Reduces cream-paper editorial feel on the homepage All-jersey-deep cards lose photographic punch (less contrast)
