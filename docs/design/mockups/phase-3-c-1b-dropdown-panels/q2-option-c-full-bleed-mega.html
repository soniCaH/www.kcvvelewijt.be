Reads as: a stadium-concourse mega-menu — every hover triggers a continuous ink band across the full viewport. Strongest visual statement of the four options. Per-panel layouts (tab-row, pill row, 3-col + accent strip) get full editorial treatment because there's room for it. The accent strip on De club is a mini brand moment ("Maar één plezante compagnie") — could become a recurring fanzine gesture across panels.

Geometry

  • width: 100% of viewport
  • position: absolute, top: 100% (flush against masthead seam)
  • Internal container max-width: 1240px (matches masthead)
  • Distinct layouts per panel: tab-row / pill-row / 3-col + accent
  • Drop shadow extends downward 16px to lift the band off content

Pros

  • Every panel gets the editorial treatment it deserves — Teams as tabs, Jeugd as ages, De club as an index.
  • Brand accent strip on De club opens a new fanzine gesture (motto, address, CTA) — could repeat for Jeugd ("Trainingstijden") or Teams ("Volgende match") later.
  • Symmetrical alignment with the masthead inner container — visually disciplined.
  • Makes the dropdown a feature of the chrome, not an afterthought.

Cons

  • Every hover paints a full-bleed ink band — visually heaviest of all options.
  • Cursor traverses across triggers and the whole band reflows — hover-grace timing becomes critical.
  • Three completely different internal layouts to maintain (tab-row / pill-row / mega-grid).
  • Brand accent strip on De club introduces a new component (essentially an editorial card inside the dropdown).
  • Highest implementation cost — full-bleed positioning, three layout variants, accent micro-component.
  • Risk of being too "corporate" — clashes with the fanzine register the locked Direction A leans into.
