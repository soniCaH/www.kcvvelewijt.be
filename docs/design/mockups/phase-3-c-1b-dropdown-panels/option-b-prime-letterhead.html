Sectie · De club

Een club met een eigen plek aan de Hollestraat

KCVV Elewijt speelt al meer dan honderd jaar in de tweede helft van het amateurvoetbal. Onder de eik aan de Hollestraat is de zaterdagochtend een gewoonte — kinderen, ouders, koffie, gevolgd door een halve liter en een match.

Reads as: a printed flyer with a section letterhead. The 4px ink band at top is the editorial-newspaper signal — "this is a labelled object" — without dropping the cream paper register. Lighter than B (no full-frame border, no hard shadow) but with the same fanzine vocabulary at the top edge. Aligns with master design §3.6 (blurred shadows for chrome).

Tokens used

  • --color-cream-soft
  • --color-paper-edge (1px sides+btm)
  • --color-ink (4px top band)
  • --shadow-soft (blurred)
  • --color-ink (text)
  • --color-ink-muted (em-dash)
  • --color-jersey-deep (hover)

Pros

  • Paper-on-paper register holds — the panel doesn't drop out of the cream world.
  • The 4px ink band signals "labelled object" — never reads as "more nav row".
  • Shadow stays blurred (chrome-licensed per master design §3.6) — softer scroll feel than B.
  • Could absorb a future tiny mono-caps section label inside the ink band ("DE CLUB") if Q3 wants it.
  • Implementation is a single border-top swap — no new tokens, no new shadow.

Cons

  • Without the ink full-frame, the panel reads slightly less "object-like" than B.
  • The 4px band may feel disproportionately heavy on Teams (4-item) panels — less so on De club.
  • Top-band-only is a less common dropdown vocabulary — risks "novel for novel's sake".
  • Panel still needs a discrete edge against busy hero content — relies on the cream-soft / cream contrast holding.

What changes if Q1 locks B′

All three submenus (Teams, Jeugd, De club) get the cream-soft + 4px ink-band-top treatment. If Q3 (Composition) later decides to add a section label, it lands inside the ink band as a small mono-caps cream string — a single editorial gesture. Animation Q4 should consider whether the ink band slides in with the panel (likely yes) or wipes in separately (probably overkill).
