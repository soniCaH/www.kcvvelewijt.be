★ 04 · TRADEOFFS — why this direction wins · what it sacrifices

↑ FOR

Native to the locked retro vocabulary — perforation is a fanzine cousin to the dashed footers on MatchCard, the mono kickers on FilterTabs, and the sharp-cornered paper-card silhouette on BrandedTabs. Tinted body + coloured icon block + right-edge claim strip carry the variant cue three ways, so colour-blind users still get shape + label + position. Italic Playfair title gives the stub editorial weight without requiring a heavier surface. No rotation keeps it functional under serious states (errors).

↓ AGAINST

The half-disc mask works only when the column bg differs from the page bg — embedding the alert inside an already-tinted card needs the stub's notch column to still resolve to a contrasting tone. The right-edge variant strip uses 6px of body width; on very narrow inline scales it competes with body padding. Phosphor Fill icons in production must use the matching weight (CheckCircle, WarningTriangle, XCircle) — the Unicode glyphs in this mockup are shape stand-ins, not the final art.
