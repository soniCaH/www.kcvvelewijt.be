Phase 6.B · Drill round 1 · `<MatchHero>`

How should the match-detail hero look?

Three structural shapes for the new <MatchHero> component on /wedstrijd/[matchId]. Each variant is rendered in both the upcoming and finished states so state-handling is judged alongside layout shape. Data is fixed: KCVV Elewijt vs RC Mechelen, 3e Provinciale A, za 14 jun · 14:30 · Sportpark Elewijt, finished score 3 — 1.

All variants respect the 6.B.d0 data-reality lock: no per-match photo, no editorial fields rendered inside the hero. The per-match preview / recap article is surfaced separately by <MatchArticleLinkCard> (drilled in 6.B.d4).

Pick one shape · later rounds refine within it
Variant A

Editorial card.

One <TapedCard> hero. Mono kicker on top, teams and score on one row, date / venue / competition mono row at the bottom. Same shape both states; only the score region swaps. Reuses every primitive we already ship — maps 1:1 onto Phase 5's <EditorialHero> if we ever consolidate.

What this gives us

  • Zero new design-system vocabulary — pure composition

  • Status badge has nowhere ceremonial to live — corner stamp is a compromise
Variant B

Ticket-stub band.

Full-bleed dark band with a perforated 'ticket-stub' left edge carrying date / venue / competition. Teams + score dominate the right side of the band. Distinctive — reads like a printed matchday ticket. Introduces one new primitive (<TicketStubBand>).

Variant C

Two-team polaroids.

Pair of taped polaroid clippings — home + away each rendered as their own card with shield + name. Big <NumberDisplay> score (or "vs") in the gap between them. Meta below. Leans hardest into the fanzine aesthetic; zero new primitive.

What this gives us

  • Most editorial-feeling option; treats the match as artefact
  • Reuses existing <TapedFigure> + <NumberDisplay> + <MonoLabelRow> primitives — zero net-new vocabulary
  • Each team gets ceremonial weight (the polaroid) — works well for cup matches, derbies, etc.

Tradeoff

  • Shield quality: PSD-sourced club crests vary in quality / brand-compliance; promoting them to polaroid scale exposes that variance
  • More vertical real estate than A or B — pushes lineup + events further down
  • Three-column layout needs a strong mobile collapse story (stack vertically with score between)
