Phase 6.B · Drill round 2 · `<MatchHero>` · A+B hybrids

Combining A's calm with B's matchday identity.

Round 1 left A (editorial card) and B (ticket-stub band) in the running; C is eliminated. This round explores three hybrid shapes that pull B's most useful idea — ticket-stub / matchday-ticket identity — into A's calm paper-card chrome without a full-bleed dark band. Each variant is rendered in both states. Data fixed: KCVV Elewijt vs RC Mechelen, 3e Provinciale A, za 14 jun · 14:30 · Sportpark Elewijt, finished score 3 — 1.

Hybrid H1

Ticket-card.

Single <TapedCard> hero, but the card is split into two zones by a perforated dashed border. Left zone (~110px) is a "ticket stub" with the big display date, time, venue. Right zone is the editorial body: kicker, teams + score, competition line. Brings B's ticket vocabulary inside A's calm card. No full-bleed dark band.

What this brings together

  • From A: calm paper card; no dark band; reads as one editorial unit
  • From B: big-display date + perforated dashed line — "this is a matchday ticket"
  • Single component (not a band wrapper) — Storybook story matches the card paradigm of every other hero

Tradeoff

  • +1 small primitive: the perforated dashed line + corner punch-out circles need to land somewhere reusable
  • Left stub eats horizontal space — the right zone gets ~110px less for teams + score
  • Mobile collapse needs thought (stub becomes top row or hides)
Hybrid H2

Card + matchday stamp.

Pure A card composition — kicker → teams + score → meta row — with one B-flavored addition: the date promoted to a small but bold matchday stamp in the upper-right corner. Like a postmark on a postcard. No perforations, no dark band, no extra primitive (it's just a styled box with display typography).

Hybrid H3

Card with ticket-footer.

A's card unchanged on top (kicker + teams + score). The bottom meta row gets promoted to a ticket-stub footer — cream-soft tint, dashed top border, punch-out circles at the corners. Reads like the perforated tear-strip at the bottom of a printed ticket. Date / venue / competition live in the footer.
