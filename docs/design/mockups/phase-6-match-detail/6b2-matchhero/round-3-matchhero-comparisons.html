How loud should the ticket separator be?

Owner picked H1 (Ticket-card) in round 2, but with a question on whether the perforation punch-outs are necessary. Round 3 compares three separator treatments of the same H1 shape — everything else identical, only the divider line between stub + body varies. Both states (upcoming + finished) rendered for each.

Treatment 1

Full perforations.

As shipped in round 2 H1: dashed divider line + two punch-out circles at the top and bottom corners of the stub. Literally reads as "this stub tears off." Strongest matchday-ticket cue.

Pros

  • Strongest "this is a matchday ticket" cue — the literal printed-ticket idiom
  • Punch-outs add visual rhythm at the corner intersections with the outer card frame

Cons

  • +1 small primitive (perforated line + circles) to ship + maintain
  • Risk of feeling fussy / over-detailed on smaller surfaces (MatchTeaser, mobile)
  • Punch-out circles need to coordinate visually with the outer card border on hover / state changes
Treatment 2

Dashed only.

Just the dashed vertical line between stub + body — no punch-out circles at all. Cleaner, calmer. The ticket-ness comes from the big display date + cream-soft stub tint + dashed divider; the literal "tear-off" cue is dropped.

0 new primitive Cleanest visually Reads more "two-zone card" than "ticket"

Pros

  • Zero new primitive — `border-right: 2px dashed` is a one-line CSS rule
  • Calmer; no fiddly corner geometry across breakpoints
  • Mobile collapse is trivial — the stub becomes a top row, dashed line becomes bottom border

Cons

  • Loses the literal ticket-stub idiom — without the punch-outs, it's just "a card with a dashed divider"
  • "Matchday ticket" identity rests entirely on the display-date typography + cream-soft tint
Treatment 3

Subtle perforations.

Compromise: the punch-out circles stay but go smaller (12px vs 18px) and switch to ink-muted instead of full ink. The dashed line also drops to 1.5px ink-muted. The ticket idiom is still present but reads as a quiet detail, not a structural element.

+1 small primitive Compromise position Subtlety risk: barely-visible on cheap monitors
