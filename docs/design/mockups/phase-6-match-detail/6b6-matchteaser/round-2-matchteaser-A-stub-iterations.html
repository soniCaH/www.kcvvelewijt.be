Why "too dense"

  • 4 information units (weekday / day / month / time) in a narrow column
  • Display-big weight makes the date typographically heavy
  • Two lines of date stack on top of the time — visual stack feels crowded
A1

Drop weekday.

Smallest change: drop the ZA prefix. Day-of-week is already implied by context (slider rows are sorted; calendar rows live under a day header). One fewer info unit in the stub, slightly more breathing room around the date.

Pros

  • Minimal change to A — same widths, same chrome, one fewer line of text
  • Date reads as one continuous unit ("14 JUN") instead of split across lines
  • Weekday is contextual data — calendar / slider rows usually provide it elsewhere

Cons

  • If the teaser appears in a non-grouped context (e.g. team page recent matches), losing the weekday means the reader has to derive it
  • Doesn't address the visual-weight density (display-big stays)
A2

Date-only stub.

Stub becomes one display-big date number stacked over a mono month label. Time moves to a kicker row in the body. Stub looks like a torn-off calendar page; body owns scheduling chrome. Centred typography.

Pros

  • Stub is the single most uncluttered of the four — one big number + a mono label
  • Most "torn calendar page" identity — leans into the editorial-paper vocabulary
  • Body can carry richer scheduling chrome (day + time + venue all in one mono row) without crowding
  • Compact reads tightly — date number stays prominent at small scale

Cons

  • Time is no longer in the stub — readers scanning slider/calendar for kickoff time have to look at the body row
  • Body grows a kicker row to absorb the time/venue — slightly taller default card
  • Compact loses the kicker row entirely (no room) — time is implicit at small scale
A3

Wider stub, mono date.

Wider stub (~100px / 80px) but the display-big date drops out entirely. Stub becomes mono caps day on top, mono caps date, mono caps time — calmer typography, more breathing room. Stub reads as a "mono tag" instead of a "ticket-stub date stamp".
