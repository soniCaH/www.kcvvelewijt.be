PHASE 3 · CHECKPOINT A · TIER C FIGURES · DIRECTION C ★ MATCHDAY PROGRAMME 2026-05-03

Het programmaboekje.

Direction C reads PlayerFigure, JerseyShirt, EndMark and QASectionDivider as pages torn from a matchday programme — rule-bordered photos, sans-mono name plates, line-art jersey contours on graph paper, and a thin double-rule with italic act-titles between Q&A clusters. Editorial sidebar treatment: quiet, paper-thin, deferential to the journalism it frames.

★ 01 · <PlayerFigure> — rule-bordered photo · line-art jersey · sans-mono name plate · on TapedCard

De matchkaart.

A single column: photo at the top inside a 1px ink rule, line-art jersey contour beneath in the same column width, name plate at the bottom reading #8 · MAXIM BREUGELMANS · MIDFIELDER. The whole stack sits on a .taped-card with a tape strip — the card carries the paper feel; the figure stays disciplined.

★ COMPOSITION PLAYERFIGURE

Drie regels, één kolom.

The photo, the jersey, and the plate are stacked rectangles of identical width — a single ink rule traces the column edge from top to bottom. The name plate's #8 renders in --color-jersey-deep so the number is the only colour spike in an otherwise monochrome card.

  • Photo: 4:5 aspect, 1px ink rule, optional PSD-2026 mono corner stamp
  • Face: circular CSS clip out of the rectangular source (clip-path: circle())
  • Jersey: 1px contour only — no fills, no shading, just outline
  • Plate: mono caps, #NN in jersey-deep, name in ink, role in ink-muted

↑ FOR

Reads instantly as a programme-booklet entry — readers recognise the matchday-list typography. The single-column rule unifies photo + jersey + plate into one composed object; the figure cannot drift apart on smaller widths. Letting the jersey live as a 1px contour means a player figure with no photo still has a recognisable artefact below.

↓ AGAINST

Quieter than tier-A or tier-B figures — the figure won't carry a hero on its own without a TapedCard rotation + tape strip around it. Three stacked rectangles with similar borders can flatten if the photo, jersey, and plate end up at the same visual weight — mitigated by the jersey-deep #NN spike in the plate.

★ 02 · MECHANICS SHEET — photo-present · photo-missing (line-art only) · photo-cropped-tight

Drie states.

PlayerFigure must survive three real states: a clean PSD photo, no photo at all, and a tight crop where the face fills most of the frame. The plate stays identical across states. Only the photo cell changes.

↑ FOR

Photo-missing state degrades to a recognisable line-art portrait — never to a generic placeholder. The plate carries the same metadata across all three states, so a roster grid mixing photo'd and un-photo'd players still reads as a single editorial artefact. Tight-crop variant gives art-direction headroom on hero spreads without forking the component.

↓ AGAINST

The line-art portrait fallback ships a small SVG library of poses — grows over time as we add youth/staff variants. Tight-crop variant requires deliberate art direction (an editor must opt-in per article); auto-cropping a wide-angle PSD photo to a tight square will produce ugly edges. Tracked as a Sanity-side art-direction concern, not a component concern.

★ 03 · <JerseyShirt> — 4-up variants · graph-paper bg · letter-overlay support

Het shirtblad.

A 4-cell row inside a single graph-paper panel (faint 8px grid via repeating-linear-gradient at ~5% opacity). Each cell is a 1px line-art jersey with optional letter overlay rendered in Playfair italic at 18% opacity behind the contour. Captions read HOME · UIT · KEEPER · U11 in mono caps below.

↑ FOR

Graph-paper backdrop is generated entirely from repeating-linear-gradient — no asset, no SVG, costs zero kilobytes. The 1px contour scales cleanly from a 32×32 navigation thumbnail to a 320×320 hero artefact without changing component code. Letter-overlay slot lets youth/transfer/keeper variants carry their own glyph (U11, GK, ★) without forking the SVG.

↓ AGAINST

Pure line-art means no shirt colour signal — a "home" jersey is indistinguishable from an "away" jersey at small sizes unless the caption reads it. We could tint the contour stroke (jersey-deep for home, ink for away) without breaking the no-fills rule; tracked as a follow-up. The graph-paper backdrop reads slightly fussy when stacked next to a TapedCard with its own paper texture.

Een laatste regel.

A 1px ink rule, broken in the middle by a mono caps tag in cream paper. The rule reads as a continuous line passing through the tag — flanked by jersey-green typographic stars. No emoji, no Lucide; just glyphs the typeface already carries.

↑ FOR

Quietest possible closer — a single 1px rule and a mono tag. The label is editor-authored so each long-form piece can sign off in its own voice (EINDE GESPREK / EINDE LOGBOEK / EINDE). Two typographic stars in jersey-deep are the only colour — same green spike used by the PlayerFigure plate and the QASectionDivider.

↓ AGAINST

So quiet that on long-scroll articles a reader can miss it — there's no large display flourish announcing "this is the end". Acceptable: the InterviewCredits block immediately below is the actual sign-off; EndMark is the pause, not the curtain.

↑ FOR

The double-rule + centered italic act-title is unmistakably a programme-booklet act break — readers slow down without being told to. Jersey-green stars at the edges add the brand colour without flooding the divider, and use a typographic glyph (✦) instead of a Lucide icon, per the visual-preferences memo.

↓ AGAINST

Two rules close together can read as a single thick band on retina-density displays at small zoom; the 14px gap between rules holds the breathing room but is sensitive to font-size adjustments. The italic act-title is editor-authored — needs editorial guidance ("keep it under 6 words") in the Sanity field.

↑ FOR

Direction C ships zero new chrome atoms. Every figure is a re-arrangement of .taped-card, .ticket-stub, .mono-label, plus .graph-paper (a 5-line CSS gradient, not a component) and a small library of 1px-stroke SVGs. Migration risk is the lowest of the three Tier C directions.

↓ AGAINST

Quiet by construction — none of the figures will carry a homepage hero on their own. They depend on their host (InterviewHero, PlayerHero, YouthBlock) to bring the editorial weight. Acceptable for body / sidebar use; if the homepage needs a louder PlayerFigure variant we add it in tier-A or tier-B, not here.

★ NOTES — direction-level summary · structure decisions · production handoff

Resolved

  • PlayerFigure is a single-column stack: photo · jersey · plate. One ink rule traces the column edge top-to-bottom.
  • Jersey illustration is 1px contour only — no fills, no shading. Same rule applies to JerseyShirt variants.
  • Photo-missing state degrades to a 1px line-art portrait — never to a generic placeholder rectangle.
  • Graph-paper background is a repeating-linear-gradient at ~5% opacity, 8px grid. No asset, no SVG.
  • EndMark uses a single 1px rule split by a mono caps tag. Two jersey-deep ★ glyphs flank the tag.
  • QASectionDivider uses two parallel 1px rules with an italic Playfair act-title between, flanked by ✦ glyphs in jersey-deep.
  • Press-down hover canon applied per memo: hover:translate-x-1 hover:translate-y-1 hover:shadow-none + transition-all duration-300.
  • No emoji in chrome — typographic glyphs only (★ ✦). Stenciletta off-limits per visual-preferences memo.
  • No radial glows, no rim-lights — chrome stays paper-thin throughout.
