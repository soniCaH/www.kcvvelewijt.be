PHASE 3 · CHECKPOINT A · TIER C FIGURES · DIRECTION B ★ STAMPED BLOCK-PRINT 2026-05-03

Een gedrukt portret.

Direction B treats every figure as a hand-pulled lino print: a hard-edged two-tone silhouette with deliberate misregistration between the ink pass, the jersey-deep underprint, and the desaturated photograph. Jerseys read as postmarks; the end-mark is a stamped star + em-dash; section dividers are jersey-green ribbons that interrupt the column like an act break.

★ 01 · PLAYERFIGURE — TapedCard(rotation="b") + TicketStub + MonoLabel composition

Een speler in twee passes.

The figure stages a desaturated rectangular photo as the ground layer, a jersey-deep "underprint" silhouette shifted 2.5px off-axis via mix-blend-mode: multiply, then the ink "overprint" outline on top. The circular face crop (per the master design doc) is composed on the rectangular source — keeping the silhouette block-flat without rim-lights or radial glows.

↑ FOR

The block-print + misregistration treatment makes the figure instantly recognisable as a KCVV editorial artefact — it cannot be mistaken for a stock photograph or a soft-shadowed product shot. Two-tone passes (ink + jersey-deep) sit inside the existing colour token map; no new chrome introduced. Composes cleanly with TapedCard rotation, TicketStub, and MonoLabel — every primitive already lives in the catalogue.

↓ AGAINST

Three layered passes (photo + underprint + overprint) are heavy at build time — every PlayerFigure ships ~1.5–2KB of inline SVG plus a desaturated raster. The 2.5px misregistration must be tuned per breakpoint or it disappears at small sizes. Editorial taste call: some readers will read the misregistration as a print bug, not a feature, until they see it on the second figure on the page.

★ 02 · PLAYERFIGURE MECHANICS — photo-present · photo-missing · photo-cropped-tight

Drie fotostates.

The same primitive accepts a rectangular psdImage, no image at all, or a tightly-cropped portrait. The block-print silhouette is the load-bearing layer: when the photo is missing, the figure still reads as a figure. Subject photos default to rectangular bounded-box crops — a circle is composed on top, never required from the source.

DEFAULT photo present
Photo present psdImage available · 2.5px misregistration · circle face crop visible
NO PHOTO illustration only
Photo missing pure block-print · crosshatch torso fill compensates for empty face circle
TIGHT portrait crop
Photo cropped tight hero/profile use · larger face circle · silhouette pulls in to follow

↑ FOR

One primitive, three resilient states. The pure-block-print fallback is not a degraded view — it's a deliberate editorial treatment, which means the ~10% of players without a clean psdImage still ship as first-class portraits. Tight-crop variant re-uses the same SVG silhouette, only the viewBox geometry shifts.

↓ AGAINST

The crosshatch fill on the no-photo state introduces a visual language (parallel lines) that no other primitive uses. Risk: a second illustrator working on Phase 4 may extend the crosshatch elsewhere and dilute it. Mitigation: lock the crosshatch as a PlayerFigure-only treatment in the component contract.

★ 03 · JERSEYSHIRT — stamped emblem · home · away · retro · training

Vier postzegels.

JerseyShirt is rendered as a perforated postage stamp. Inside the frame: the same two-tone block print as PlayerFigure — jersey-deep underprint shifted 2.5px down-left of the ink overprint. A circular postmark sits upper-right with mono caps metadata. Letter-overlay support paints a Freight Display 900 glyph across the chest in cream with an ink stroke.

↑ FOR

The postmark frame solves an old problem: jerseys floating on cream lose their silhouette at thumbnail size. Wrapped in a perforated stamp, every jersey reads at WebshopStrip dimensions and at full hero scale. The circular postmark slot is a free metadata channel — season, audience, edition — without introducing a new label primitive.

↓ AGAINST

Postmark + perforated dashed inner border + outer ink border = a lot of edges per cell. On a 4-up WebshopStrip the page reads "busy". Counter-pattern: the postmark is optional — drop it on dense lists, keep it on hero / featured contexts only.

★ 04 · ENDMARK — centered, large jersey-deep star + em-dash + italic mono "EINDE"

Het einde van een gesprek.

A Freight Display 900 star in jersey-deep flanked by a 56px ink em-dash and an italic mono caps "EINDE GESPREK". Nothing rotates. Nothing glows. The mark is structural — it tells the reader the act has closed and the column has stopped.

★ Einde gesprek ★

↑ FOR

Three glyphs, zero new tokens. Star uses Freight Display 900 (already in the font stack), dash is a 56×4 ink rectangle, label is mono italic. Reads at the bottom of an interview the way a stamped end-mark used to terminate a printed feature.

↓ AGAINST

Symmetric (star + dash · label · dash + star) is heavier than the minimal "* Einde gesprek *" the master design doc spec'd. Asymmetric variant (single star + single dash + label) is the fallback for narrow contexts; the symmetric form is the hero variant.

★ 05 · QASECTIONDIVIDER — full-width rotated jersey-green ribbon · cream italic title slot

Een act break.

Q&A clusters need a divider that interrupts the column without breaking the cream paper foundation. Direction B uses a rotated jersey-deep ribbon — a MonoLabel scaled to full-width, tilted -1.6°, with notched cream corners that fake a stamped paper banner. Cream italic Playfair display sets the act title across it; mono kicker on the left names the act number.

↑ FOR

The ribbon is unmistakably the redesign's voice — jersey-deep field, cream italic, mono kicker, slight rotation, ink border. Every chrome ingredient is locked in the token map. Two ribbons in alternating rotations (-1.6° / +1.2°) keep a long interview from feeling mechanical.

↓ AGAINST

Rotated full-width band creates a tiny vertical gap above and below — at narrow breakpoints the column reflows around the corners. The notched cream corners are decorative; they have to survive zoom and high-contrast modes (planned: drop the corners in prefers-contrast: more).

★ NOTES — direction-level summary, production handoff

Resolved

  • PlayerFigure renders as 2-tone block-print: photo (desaturated) + jersey-deep underprint + ink overprint, with 2.5px misregistration via mix-blend-mode: multiply
  • Three photo states: present (default), missing (pure block-print + crosshatch), cropped-tight (larger face circle + zoomed silhouette)
  • JerseyShirt is a perforated postage-stamp emblem with a circular postmark slot upper-right; letter-overlay uses Playfair Display 900 with an ink stroke shim
  • EndMark is a symmetric Freight Display 900 star + 56×4 ink dash + italic mono "EINDE GESPREK"; asymmetric narrow-context variant available
  • QASectionDivider is a full-width jersey-deep ribbon, rotated -1.6° / +1.2° alternately, with notched cream corner accents and cream italic Playfair display title
  • Press-down hover canon (hover:shadow-none + translate(4px, 4px) + transition-all 300ms) applies to every interactive paper-stamped wrapper
  • Stenciletta is not used. No radial glows, no rim-lights. Block-print is print-flat.
  • Subject photos default to rectangular bounded-box crops; the circular face is composed on top via clip-path
