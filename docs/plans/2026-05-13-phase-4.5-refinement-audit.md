# Phase 4.5 — Homepage Refinement Audit

**Date:** 2026-05-13
**Owner:** @climacon
**Status:** Open — drilling per section, hero first

## Context

After Phase 4 (#1526) shipped, the owner reviewed the live homepage against the
original retro-terrace-fanzine mockups and felt the final implementation drifted
visually. A free-form refinement document was authored:

- Brief: `docs/design/kcvv-elewijt-design-refinement.md`
- Visual reference: `docs/design/Untitled_Panorama-1.jpg` (a panorama composite
  closely related to the canonical mockups under
  `docs/design/mockups/retro-terrace-fanzine/`)
- Stated comparison target: `full_fanzine.jpeg` (not in repo at audit time)

This plan captures the gap analysis between the brief, the canonical
locked-design artefacts (`docs/design/mockups/phase-4-homepage/*-locked.md`),
and the current implementation under `apps/web/src/components/home/`.

## Verdict

The brief reads as a **redesign brief disguised as a refinement** — it bundles
~15 discrete design decisions, several of which directly **override Phase 4
locks** (NewsGrid layout, hero carousel, section order). The panorama
re-attached as the "target" is the same source family that informed the locked
mockups — gaps the owner perceives are partially explained by the locked
trade-offs made during Phase 4's per-round drill-down.

To avoid a second "refinement document" two months from now, the owner has
chosen to **drill per section** rather than override locks wholesale or ship
the brief as written.

## B. Ambiguities the brief does not resolve

### B1. Diagonal stripe band — new primitive or `<StripedSeam>` recipe?

We already ship `<StripedSeam>` (Phase 0) and `--pattern-jersey-stripes-*`
tokens. Brief describes a ~24px-tall full-bleed stripe band that may be a new
`<StripedSeam>` colour/height variant or a fundamentally different primitive.
Resolve before authoring the youth-section top frame and clubshop top/bottom
frame.

### B2. Horizontal-stripe meta-panel accent depends on a field that does not exist

Brief §6 (Announcement card) — the meta panel gets a horizontal-stripe accent
**"for tag-driven subcategories (jeugd-victory, column)"**. The Sanity
`article` schema (`packages/sanity-schemas/src/article.ts`) has only
`articleType` (4 enum values) + `featured` (boolean). No `tags`, `category`,
or `subcategory` field exists.

Resolution options:

- Add a `subcategory` enum to the schema (migration cost — contradicts Phase 4
  IA-lock's "Sanity migrations required: 0").
- Drop the horizontal-stripe accent.
- Redefine the trigger (e.g. articleType=announcement always gets the accent).

### B3. Clubshop: no photos, but illustrations either?

Brief §10 — "no product images, … don't fake it with stock imagery." But the
panorama itself shows 4 illustrated jerseys (H / U / KCVV / R). Per
`feedback_visual_preferences`, we already discarded the panorama's U11
illustration on the youth section. Same standard for clubshop jerseys → drop
the jersey row entirely? Or keep one small `<JerseyShirt>`-style flourish?

### B4. Card backgrounds: per-articleType or per-slot?

- Current `NewsGrid` cycles backgrounds **per slot**
  (`cream / jersey-deep / cream-soft / ink / cream`) for paper-stamp rhythm
  variety.
- Brief: per-`articleType` (green = transfer, cream = interview / announcement
  / event).
- These encode different design intents (rhythm vs semantics). One has to win.

### B5. Transfer directional chips

Brief §3 — `IN ↓ / OUT ↑ / VERLENGING ↻`. The ↻ glyph for "verlenging" is
unusual; needs a prototype against `feedback_no_decorative_nav_ornaments`
(functional indicator OK; decorative ornament not).

### B6. Hero — Person portrait thumbnail next to headline

Brief §3 (Interview / Transfer) — "if a referenced Person has a portrait in
Sanity, show as a small thumbnail next to the headline." Per
`feedback_subject_photo_fallback`, ~90% of players only have rectangular
`psdImage` (no cutouts). Per `feedback_playerfigure_no_hybrid`, no photo +
drawing hybrid. Define the visual treatment — TapedFigure? Polaroid? Inline
pill?

## E. Resolution plan

Per owner choice (2026-05-13), we drill per section with `/design-an-interface`
rounds. **Each round produces locked mockups + a `<surface>-locked.md` next to
the Phase 4 locks**, then implementation lands as a separate PR.

### Suggested round order

1. **Round R1 — Hero placement (A1).** Carousel vs single + featured-row
   (Uitgelicht) variants. This is the upstream decision; A3 collapses into it.
2. **Round R2 — NewsGrid geometry (A2).** Locked 1+4 vs proposed 3×2 vs hybrid.
3. **Round R3 — Per-card semantics vs rhythm (B4).** Once R2 is locked.
4. **Round R4 — Section spine ordering (A4).** Sponsors-as-close vs
   clubshop-as-close.
5. **Round R5 — Youth top frame + dual CTA (A5, B1).**
6. **Round R6 — Clubshop framing & jersey treatment (B3, B1).**
7. **Round R7 — Schema-trigger decision for horizontal-stripe accent (B2).**
   May not need a visual round — could be a pure schema decision after R3.

### Low-risk PR (parallel to rounds)

Items D1–D5 above can ship as one PR while the rounds run. They don't conflict
with any pending lock decision.

## I. Data constraints surfaced during R1.5

### I1. Hero images are landscape-only (owner constraint, 2026-05-13)

The Sanity `article.coverImage` field is ALWAYS uploaded in landscape
orientation. There are no portrait or square crops. This invalidates
mockups in earlier rounds that used 4:5 portrait crops for Interview and
Transfer heroes (`InterviewHero.tsx:189`, `TransferHero.tsx:155` — both
ship `aspect-[4/5]` on the detail page).

**Implication for detail-page heroes:** the existing `InterviewHero` and
`TransferHero` 4:5 portrait projections (built via Sanity hotspot crop
of a landscape source) are working but optional. The homepage hero
should NOT request portrait crops — it uses the landscape source
directly.

**Implication for homepage hero (R1.5):** all per-articleType mockups
use a single landscape (3:2 or 16:9) taped polaroid in the right column.
Person photos (`player.psdImage` / `transparentImage`) are separate from
the hero focal point and may appear as small inline credit thumbnails
(~16px) but never as the hero image itself.
